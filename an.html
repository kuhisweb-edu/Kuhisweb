Our History & Achievements

Celebrating over six decades of academic excellence and contributions to Ghanaian education

Historical Timeline

Milestones in the journey of Kumasi High School since its establishment

1964

Foundation & Establishment

Kumasi High School started as a private secondary known as Kwame Nkrumah Secondary School. It was established in 1962 by the late S.K. Amoah in rented premises at Asuoyeboah, a suburb of Kumasi. In 1963/64 academic year, it moved to Kwadaso and was housed in rented premises at the present day Peter's Educational Centre.

1965

It's Transition

The then Kwame Nkrumah Secondary School which was a mixed institution was absorbed into the public school system by the Ghana Government in the 1965/66 academic year with Mr. Albert Appiah, formerly of Prempeh College as the first Headmaster.


After the 1966 military coup which ousted President Kwame Nkrumah's regime, the Board of Governors of the then Kwame Nkrumah Secondary School, sought the approval of the Ghana Education service and changed the School's name to Kumasi High School.

2000

The Arise

An analysis of SSSCE results for science students (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Elective Mathematics) from 1996 to 2001 showed that the 2000 year group performed below the 1999 and 1996 cohorts but better than the 1998 and 1997 groups. Around this period, the school was cementing its reputation, following a strong performance record in the early to mid-1990s..
